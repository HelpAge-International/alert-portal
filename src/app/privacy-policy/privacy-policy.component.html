The ALERT platform is committed to safeguarding your safety online. Please read the following policy to understand how your personal information will be treated.

1. What personally identifiable information is collected from you?

The ALERT platform collects the following information regarding users of the platform: when volunteered by you, full name, email address, job title, phone number, department and user type.

If you would like to stop using the ALERT platform and for us to delete all personal data we have on file, please contact us at info@alertpreparedness.org.

2. How is your information used?

The ALERT platform uses the information it collects about you to undertake the preparedness functions, for example, alerting all ERT members when a hazard changes from green to amber, alerting those allocated to MPAs or APAs when they become out of date, etc.

3. Who is collecting your information?

The ALERT platform is the sole owner of the information collected on this site. We will not sell, share, or rent this information to third parties.

4. With whom does the ALERT platform share your information?

The ALERT platform is committed to protecting the personal data of its users. We will always work to fully protect your rights and comply with our obligations under the GDPR and the Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003.

You may request a copy of the personal information relating to you that is kept on file by the ALERT platform by writing to info@alertpreparedness.org. We cannot be held responsible for the privacy of data collected by websites not owned managed by the ALERT platform.

5. What are my choices regarding collection, use and distribution of my information?

The ALERT platform will not disclose any of your personally identifiable information, except where you have given your explicit permission. We may also need to disclose your information if required to do so by law.

6. What is the ALERT platform policy on allowing me to update, correct or delete my personally identifiable information?

The accuracy of your individual identifying information is important to the ALERT platform. If you change your email address, or if any of the other information we hold is inaccurate or out of date, please email: info@alertpreparedness.org

7. What security precautions are in place to protect the loss, misuse or alteration of my information?

When you give the ALERT platform personal information we take steps to ensure that your information is treated securely. Non-sensitive details (your email address etc.) may be transmitted normally over the Internet, and this can never be guaranteed to be 100% secure. As a result, while we strive to protect your personal information, the ALERT platform cannot ensure or warrant the security of any information you transmit to us, and you do so at your own risk. Once we receive your transmission, the ALERT platform is committed to taking reasonable steps to ensure its security on our systems. The ALERT platform is hosted on Google Firebase which uses various security measures in order to prevent loss, misuse or alteration of data. The Security Measures include measures to encrypt personal data; to help ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability and resilience of Google's systems and services; to help restore timely access to personal data following an incident; and for regular testing of effectiveness. Google may update or modify the Security Measures from time to time provided that such updates and modifications do not result in the degradation of the overall security of the Services.

If Google becomes aware of a Data Incident, Google will: (a) notify Customer of the Data Incident promptly and without undue delay after becoming aware of the Data Incident; and (b) promptly take reasonable steps to minimise harm and secure Customer Data. Notifications made pursuant to this section will describe, to the extent possible, details of the Data Incident, including steps taken to mitigate the potential risks and steps Google recommends Customer take to address the Data Incident.

In the event the ALERT team receives such notification, this will be forwarded on to all users of the platform, including all details of the data incident.

8. Does the ALERT platform use cookies on its website?

Yes. Cookies are pieces of information that are sent to your computer by the ALERT platform when you log on to our website. They are stored on your computer's hard drive, allowing us to recognise you as a user when you next visit.

If you do not want cookies to be stored on your computer, it is possible for you to disable this function. However, if you choose to do this, you may be unable to access certain parts of our site.

9. Does the ALERT platform review its privacy policy?

The ALERT platform reserves the right to amend this privacy policy
